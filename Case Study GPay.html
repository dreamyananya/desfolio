Senior friendly GooglePay

View on Behance

Role➔ Product Designer

Duration➔ 4 weeks

Tools➔ Figma, Miro, GPay [reference]

Elevator summary➔ A UX case study on integrating a senior-friendly mode into UPI apps to improve accessibility, trust, and ease of use.

Problem➔

Senior citizens (55+, especially 65+) often struggle to complete essential digital financial tasks such as UPI payments, hospital registrations, and online bookings without assistance. Despite the availability of UPI apps, many seniors abandon onboarding or avoid digital payments entirely due to complex flows, security confusion, and interface overload.

Objective➔

Design a simplified Senior Mode interaction layer within a UPI app that enables seniors to independently perform essential transactions while improving clarity, trust, and usability.

  • Research method➔
  • Semi-structured user interviews (10 Seniors with different level of digital literacy)
  • Competitive analysis of existing platforms like GPay, PhonePay, PayTM
  • Task walkthroughs with seniors
  • Insight highlights➔
  • Complex, time-bound onboarding flows (OTP, KYC, PIN setup) are the single biggest barrier preventing seniors from ever completing UPI adoption
  • Feature-dense “super-app” interfaces overwhelm seniors cognitively, causing errors and anxiety even when basic payment capability exists
  • Unexplained security prompts (OTP, PIN, biometrics) increase fear of fraud among seniors, reducing trust and willingness to transact
  • Unclear error messages and weak recovery guidance lead seniors to permanently abandon apps rather than attempt reuse
  • Focus Areas➔
  • Onboarding experience
  • Payment task completion
  • Trust and fraud perception
  • Error handling

User Flow➔

  • Senior Mode Concept➔
  • A dedicated Senior Mode simplifies the app experience by focusing only on the most common tasks.
  • Simplified home screen➔
  • Pay
  • Check Balance
  • Transfer Money
  • UPI ID + QR Code
  • Common pin for app lock and payment gateway➔
  • Reduces cognitive load by eliminating the need to remember multiple credentials
  • Increases security by ensuring that the same PIN is used for both app access and transaction authorization
  • Enhances user confidence and trust in the app's security measures
  • Option to set a separate PIN for app lock and payment gateway available under advanced settings

Final prototype➔

  • Impact➔
  • Reduced cognitive load for senior users
  • Improved confidence and task completion
  • Increased trust in digital transactions
  • Reflection➔
  • Learned the importance of designing for emotional safety
  • Understood the value of restraint in feature design
  • Gained insights into accessibility and inclusivity in UX
