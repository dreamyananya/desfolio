Refining GPay | Senior-Friendly UPI Experience


View on Behance

ROLE: Product Designer

TOOLS: Figma, Miro, GPay [reference]

DURATION: 4 Weeks

ELEVATOR SUMMARY: A UX case study on integrating a senior-friendly mode into UPI apps to improve accessibility, trust, and ease of use.


Problem & Objective

PROBLEM: Senior citizens (age 55+ and especially 65+) struggle to independently complete essential digital tasks such as banking, UPI payments, hospital registrations, lab report checks, and appointment bookings.

OBJECTIVE: Design a simplified, safe, and highly intuitive ‚ÄúSenior Mode‚Äù interaction layer that enables senior citizens to confidently use essential digital service - UPI, without external assistance.

Research & Insight

RESEARCH METHODS:

  • User interviews (Seniors with different level of digital literacy)
  • Competitive audit of existing platforms like GPay, PhonePay, PayTM

INSIGHT HIGHLIGHTS:

  • Complex, time-bound onboarding flows (OTP, KYC, PIN setup) are the single biggest barrier preventing seniors from ever completing UPI adoption.
  • Feature-dense ‚Äúsuper-app‚Äù interfaces overwhelm seniors cognitively, causing errors and anxiety even when basic payment capability exists.
  • Unexplained security prompts (OTP, PIN, biometrics) increase fear of fraud among seniors, reducing trust and willingness to transact.
  • Unclear error messages and weak recovery guidance lead seniors to permanently abandon apps rather than attempt reuse.

User Flow at Glance

FROM : Guiding users while onboarding.

Design Highlights

Designed to reduce anxiety and confusion, Senior Citizen Mode focuses on what seniors need most - clear actions, reassurance, and control.

Impact & Reflections

IMPACT (POTENTIAL):

  • Reduced cognitive load for senior users.
  • Improved confidence and task completion.
  • Increased trust in digital transactions.


REFLECTION:

  • Learned the importance of designing for emotional safety.
  • Understood the value of restraint in feature design.
  • Gained insights into accessibility and inclusivity in UX.
